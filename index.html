The Problem With How Most People Use AI

You're not getting bad output because the AI is bad. You're getting bad output because vague prompts produce vague results.

What you type
"Write me a blog post about productivity."
What you get back
A generic 5-paragraph essay that sounds like it was written by someone who has never had a job, starting with "In today's fast-paced world..."

The fix isn't "be more specific." It's knowing exactly what specific looks like for each type of task. That's what this guide is.

Before vs. After — Real Example

Workflow W001: Turn scattered notes into a polished blog post

✗ What most people type
"Turn my notes into a blog post: [pastes messy bullet points]"
Result: A mechanical listicle. Reads like your notes got a light grammar pass — no voice, no flow, no hook.
✓ What you should type
"You are a professional content strategist. I'm going to give you a brain-dump of rough notes on [TOPIC]. Transform them into a complete, well-structured post of [WORD COUNT] for [TARGET READER]. Open with a hook that speaks to their frustration, use clear H2 subheadings, write in a [TONE] voice. Synthesize — don't reorganize. Notes: [PASTE HERE]"
Result: An actual article with narrative flow, a strong opening, and a conclusion that doesn't repeat the intro.
